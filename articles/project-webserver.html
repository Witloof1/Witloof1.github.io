Project: hosting my own website

Recently I decided to step up my game and create my personal website. Aimed at introducing myself towards companies and interested people, I envisioned a clean and modern site. To give myself an extra challenge, I decided to host the site myself. Unbekownst to me, chaos was about to ensue.

Calm before the storm

I decided to buy myself a Raspberry Pi. A cheap microcontroller with networking capabilities seemed the perfect fit. But wait ... Didn't I have an old Macbook Air lying in one of my closets? Aha! Now I even didn't need to pay for hardware. I can just host my website on that old Macbook Air. Just install Debian on that damn thang and we're all set.

The chaos

I downloaded the ISO image, found an old USB stick, downloaded Balena-Etcher and I was on my way to create a bootable USB. God darn it, flash failed. The first part of the chaos. Okay, try it again. Still failed. Try another USB? Still failed. Reformat USB, reinstall Balena-Etcher? Still nothing. Eventually I just ditched Balena-Etcher altogheter. After trying - and failing - with Ubuntu's Startup Disk Creator, I found a terminal command, tried it, and it magically worked.

Okay, an hour wasted already, but let's just move on, we're almost there. I booted from the USB and followed the installer. "No Ethernet card detected. If you know the name of the driver needed by your Ethernet card, you can select it from the list." Uh-oh... I don't know what Ethernet card I have, and moments later I realised the one I should have wasn't even in the list. Let's just skip that part for now.

After some waiting Debian was installed. But, of course, there was no internet connection. Installing packages is quite difficult without connection and hosting a website even impossible. I knew what I had to do, but I didn't know if I had the strength to do it. The difficult task ahead of me was to painstakingly download the driver packages and all its dependencies via one device onto a USB, mount the USB on the Macbook Air, and install the packages from that USB. Using apt-rdepends doing this seemed surprisingly easy. Or so it seemed. After the first try, a number of dependencies failed to install, ultimately resulting in the driver package failing to install. What followed was a tedious fight against a dependency hell. Numerous trips of my USB From my laptop to the Macbook Air and working on a system that even didn't have vim, but vi. Those were the consequences of choosing to host a website on a Debian installation on a Macbook Air.

After rain comes sunshine

Hours were wasted in that battle, but I prevailed. Triumph was ahead of me. Finally, I had an internet connection. Nginx and cerbot were installed, and the rest is history. The result is a dusty and old - but more than capable - Macbook Air serving you this page. You're welcome.

Update: the website is now no longer self-hosted, because my father unplugged the cable.
